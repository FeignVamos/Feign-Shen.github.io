Research

My research is primarily focused on the following areas:

  • Energy Conversion and Harvesting: Developing novel methods for harvesting and converting energy at the micro/nano scale, with applications in self-powered devices.
  • Biomedical Sensors: Designing and optimizing wearable piezoelectric sensors for real-time health monitoring, particularly in detecting physiological signals.
  • Micro/Nano Systems: Investigating microfluidic devices and their integration with energy harvesting systems for lab-on-a-chip applications.
  • Machine Learning: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to enhance the performance and functionality of micro/nano devices, particularly in optimizing sensor data processing.
I am also exploring the intersection of these areas, focusing on creating energy-efficient systems that can power advanced biomedical devices.

For more details on my research contributions, please visit my Google Scholar profile.

Publications
